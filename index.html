Why LATAM importers have no alternative

Cross-border USD financing doesn't exist for LATAM companies. We built it.

Banks in LATAM won't finance US imports in USD without local collateral. US suppliers don't extend credit to foreign companies without local history. Informal lenders charge 36-60%+ annualized. MasterStock takes that credit risk because we are on the balance sheet on both sides of the deal, with inventory as collateral.

36–60% Annualized cost of alternative financing for LATAM importers (informal lenders). MasterStock's cost is embedded in the FOB price.
Why now

The market opened. Most missed it.

The US → LATAM corridor has a structural imbalance: too much inventory on the US side, too much unfinanced demand on the LATAM side. Four forces are pushing it to a breaking point.

  • US overstock at multi-year highs

    Tariff front-loading in 2025 and the post-COVID inventory correction left Northeast retailers and 3PLs with excess stock. The lot pipeline has never been deeper.

  • China tariffs are reshaping LATAM sourcing

    Trump tariffs on Chinese imports shrank the cheap-China catalog for LATAM buyers. Importers are looking for alternatives. US overstock is the answer, if they can finance it.

  • LATAM demand for US brands at a record

    A strong dollar and US brand aspiration keep pushing average tickets up. Tier-1 importers in Chile, Colombia, Mexico, and Peru have the demand but not access to financed inventory.

  • Zero institutional capital in the corridor

    US banks won't lend to LATAM importers without local history. LATAM banks won't finance USD imports without local collateral. This gap isn't going away on its own.
