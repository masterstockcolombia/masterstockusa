Our story

Why we built the credit layer cross-border trade was missing.

MasterStock USA started with two observations. First: buyers outside the US were paying retail prices for inventory sitting in US warehouses, marked down 70%. Second: even when they could access it, they had to tie up 100% of the capital upfront — US banks don't extend trade credit to LatAm importers without local history, and LatAm banks charge local rates on USD they don't hold. We built the layer that closes both gaps: we pay the US supplier in cash, ship the LatAm importer with Net-30 terms, and score every lot against their buyer profile. Each deal feeds our proprietary underwriting model.

The problem became obvious.

Jose and Esteban were running cross-border deals from a desk in Latin America. Every buyer asked the same thing: where can I get vetted US lots without losing a week to auctions?

MasterStock USA Inc., Delaware.

Incorporated, US Mercury Treasury opened, first three suppliers signed. First lot shipped to a Tier-1 import buyer in March.

Crossed the operator threshold.

100+ active US suppliers in conversation, recurring buyers in 14 US states. Same paper, same paperwork, same Bill of Sale across every deal.

Operator, not platform.

We hold inventory, sign the BoS, take recourse risk. That's why buyers come back for the second deal, and the tenth.

The problem we picked

Buyers shouldn't have to gamble on what's in the pallet.

Most B2B liquidation channels are designed for the seller. Mystery manifests, no recourse, 50-bidder auctions, brokers who disappear after pickup. We built the opposite: vetted lots, full manifests, real warehouse photos, named reps, written recourse.

Mystery pallets, no manifests

Auction sites hide SKUs behind a category label and a photo. You bid blind. We send full SKU-level manifests before any deposit.

Channel conflicts hidden until pickup

Brand restrictions, MAP policies, geo-exclusions, none of it disclosed up front. We screen for channel fit before we even show you the lot.

No recourse when condition misses

"As-is, where-is" is the industry default. We put written recourse in the Bill of Sale: variance above 5% triggers pro-rated credit, resolved in one week.

Brokers vanish post-wire

Once the money lands, you're alone. We answer the phone after the deal closes, same number, same two people, every time.

Credentials

What we put in writing, before you wire a dollar.

A buyer's risk in this category is almost entirely paperwork. Here's what we sign, what we file, and what we name on every transaction, before you transfer funds.

Mercury Treasury wire-out
